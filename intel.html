Lamborghini, often referred to as "Lambo," is an iconic Italian luxury sports car manufacturer founded by Ferruccio Lamborghini in 1963. Originally established to compete with Ferrari, the brand quickly gained a reputation for its bold, avant-garde designs and high-performance vehicles. Lamborghini's lineup includes legendary models like the Miura, Countach, and Aventador, each showcasing innovative engineering and striking aesthetics. Known for their powerful V12 engines and distinctive sharp lines, Lamborghinis symbolize luxury, speed, and exclusivity, making them a favorite among car enthusiasts and collectors worldwide. Today, Lamborghini continues to push the boundaries of automotive design and technology, embodying the spirit of Italian craftsmanship and performance.
